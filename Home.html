Map Introduction

Sri Lanka is known as the "Pearl of the Indian Ocean" and It has a tropical weather. During the old days, it is branded as the "Ceylon".There are 9 Provinces and 26 Districts in Sri Lanka.They Central, Northern, Southern, Eastern, Western, North Central, Uva, Sabaragamuwa and North Western. Each provnice is under the govern of a certain amount Districts.

Kandy, Matale and Nuwara Eliya are districts which belong to Central Province. Northen Province governs Jaffna, Kilinochi, Mullativ, Vavuniya and Mannar districts. Hambanthota, Matara and Galle districts are under the Southern Province. Eastern Province rules the Trincomalee, Batticaloe and Ampara districts. Gampaha, Colombo and Kaluthara are districts which belong to Western Province.North Central Province control Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa districts. Badulla and Monaragala are districts which belong to Uva Province. Sabaragamuwa Province rules the Kegalle and Rattnapaura districts. Kurunagala and Putthalama districts are ruled by the North Western Province.

Divisional Secretarit are under the authority of a district. Kandy, Matale and Nuwara Eliya Districts have 18, 11 and 5 divisional secretarit accordingly. 15, 4, 6, 4 and 6 divisional secretarits are governed by Jaffna, Kilinochi, Mullativ, Vavuniya and Mannar districts respectively. Hambanthota, Matara and Galle districts control 12, 16, and 22 divisional secretarits accordingly. 11, 14 and 20 divisional secretarits are ruled by Trincomalee, Batticaloe and Ampara districts respectively. Gampaha, Colombo and Kaluthara govern 13, 13 and 14 divisional secretarits accordingly. 22 and 7 divisional secretarits are controlled by Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa districts respectively. Badulla and Monaragala districts rule 15 and 12 divisional secretarits accordingly. Kegalle and Rattnapura districts control 11 and 17 divisional secretarits respectively.
